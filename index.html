FROM THE FIELD

Built with the people who use it.

GETTING STARTED

From first call to first dashboard in four steps.

STEP 01

Program mapping

We sit with your M&E team and map your cascade, indicators, populations, and reporting lines. Roughly two weeks, mostly conversations.

STEP 02

Configure & migrate

Your modules are configured and historical data is migrated from spreadsheets — cleaned, validated, and preserved. Nothing is lost.

STEP 03

Train the field

Hands-on training for managers and health workers — in-app guides, video tutorials, and a real human on the other end of the chat.

STEP 04

Go live & grow

Launch site by site with live dashboards from day one. Add modules as your program expands — the platform grows with you.

QUESTIONS

Frequently asked questions

What is HIVnext and who is it for? +

HIVnext is a health information platform for HIV programs run by NGOs, community organisations, and national program teams. It replaces fragile spreadsheet workflows with a structured, cloud-based system covering outreach, testing, linkage to care, prevention, and program monitoring — designed for differentiated HIV services delivered by community health workers.

Is HIVnext proven in a real national program? +

Yes. HIVnext supports Malaysia's differentiated HIV services data ecosystem across 21 implementing partners (NGOs), more than 50 service sites, and 200+ field workers — in partnership with the Malaysian AIDS Foundation and the Ministry of Health. HIVnext Legacy improved dashboard load times by 80%.

Can it match our country's national indicators and reporting? +

That's the core design principle. During program mapping we configure your cascade, key population definitions, national indicators, and funder reporting formats (Global Fund, PEPFAR, national health ministry templates). The platform adapts to your framework — not the other way around.

What about client confidentiality and data security? +

HIVnext uses role-based and attribute-based access control (RBAC + ABAC), so every user sees only the data their role and site permit. Client data is held in structured cloud infrastructure with audit trails — built around the confidentiality standards HIV services demand for key populations.

We're a small NGO with limited budget. Is this for us? +

Yes — HIVnext was built by community for community, starting from exactly that reality. The modular design means you start with what you need now and expand later. Talk to us about your program size and funding context; the onboarding path is shaped around NGO and donor procurement realities, not enterprise sales cycles.

How long does implementation take? +

A typical deployment runs eight to twelve weeks from program mapping to first sites live: two weeks of mapping, four to six weeks of configuration and data migration, then training and phased go-live. Timelines flex with program complexity and the state of your existing data.
